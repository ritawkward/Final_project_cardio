Introduction

Cardiovascular disease remains the leading cause of death in the United States, and heart disease mortality poses a significant burden across diverse populations. To better understand the distribution of this health outcome, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) provides the Heart Disease Mortality Data Among U.S. Adults (35+) by State, Territory, and County, 20182020. This dataset contains age-adjusted, spatially smoothed three-year average mortality rates per 100,000 population. The data are stratified by demographic factors such as sex and race/ethnicity, and reported at multiple geographic levels including county, state, and national aggregates.The objective of this analysis is to describe the distribution of heart disease mortality across demographic and geographic groups, to assess whether mortality rates differ significantly by race/ethnicity, sex, and location, to examine the patterns, and to explore modeling approaches that identify factors associated with elevated mortality risk.

  • By geographic level, most of the data were reported at the county level (97%), with relatively few entries at the state or national levels. These findings suggest disparities in mortality burden by race/ethnicity and highlight the predominance of county-level reporting in the dataset.
